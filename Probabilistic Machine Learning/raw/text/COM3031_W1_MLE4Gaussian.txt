COM3031 Probabilistic Machine Learning

Problem Setup
Let D = {y1, y2, . . . , yN} be a set of independent and identically distributed samples drawn from a
univariate Gaussian distribution yi ∼N(µ, σ2), where µ and σ2 are unknown parameters.
The objective of Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE) is to find parameter values that
maximise the likelihood function.

Interpretation
• MLE chooses parameters that make the observed data most probable.
• For a Gaussian: Best mean = centre of the data and Best variance = average squared deviation
• This links directly to least squares and later to MAP estimation when priors are added
