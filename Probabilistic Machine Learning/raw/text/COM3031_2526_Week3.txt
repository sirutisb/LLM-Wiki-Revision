The Core Idea of Approximate 
Inference
Instead of computing the exact posterior: рқ‘қ(рқңғвҲЈрқ’ҹ), we approximate it with a 
simpler distribution: рқ‘қ(рқңғвҲЈрқ’ҹ) вүҲрқ‘һ(рқңғ)
Main families of approximate inference
вҖў Local approximations
вҖў Approximate the posterior near its peak
вҖў Example: Laplace approximation
вҖў Optimisation-based methods
вҖў Find the вҖңbestвҖқ approximating distribution
вҖў Example: Variational inference
вҖў Sampling-based methods
вҖў Draw samples from the posterior
вҖў Example: MCMC

--- page 18 ---
Summary of Laplace Approx.
Works well when:
вҖў Posterior is unimodal
вҖў Posterior is approximately symmetric
вҖў Data size is moderate to large
вҖў Laplace is the simplest bridge from optimisation to Bayesian uncertainty.
Works poorly when:
вҖў Posterior is multimodal
вҖў Posterior is highly skewed
вҖў Strong non-linearities (e.g. deep neural networks)
вҖў Laplace is local вҖ” it only sees the peak, and may be limited to capturing important 
global perspectives.
