## Summary

Introduces the module structure and answers the question "what is Nature-Inspired Computation?" by decomposing the title into its three components: Nature, Inspired, and Computation.

## Key content

### What is NIC?
- **Nature:** which natural systems? Evolution, Brains, Collective behaviours (ants, swarms)
- **Inspired:** why use nature? Evolution produced the most complex objects we know (ourselves); brains solve perception effortlessly; collective behaviours show unintelligent individuals → intelligent group
- **Computation:** what problems? Optimisation and learning problems too hard for exact methods

## Key takeaways
- NIC is about borrowing design principles from nature, not simulating it
- Three natural systems → three families of NIC algorithms: evolutionary, neural, swarm
- Problems addressed are typically NP-hard optimisation or learning tasks
