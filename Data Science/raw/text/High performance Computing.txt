--- page 6 ---
Amdahl’s Law
Predicts the theoretical speedup when using 
multiple processors
•
The speedup is bounded by the sequential part of 
a computation that cannot be parallelised
•
Slatency is the theoretical speedup of the execution 
of the whole task;
•
s is the speedup of the part of the task that 
benefits from improved system resources;
•
p is the proportion of execution time that the part 
benefiting from improved resources originally 
occupied.

Amdahl’s Law
Ex: Workload: sum of 10 scalars (sequential), and 
10 × 10 matrix sum (parallel)
•
Single processor: 
Time = (10 + 100) × tadd
•

Resource Management
•
Resource allocation takes care of assigning physical hardware, which may span from a 
fraction of the machine to the entire system, to specific user tasks based on their 
requirements. Resource managers typically recognize the following resource types.
•
Compute nodes
•
Processing cores
•

•

•

--- page 12 ---
Resource Management
• Resource managers allocate the available computing resources to jobs specified by users.
•
A job is a self-contained work unit with associated input data that during its execution produces 
some output result data.
•
Jobs may be monolithic or subdivided into a number of smaller steps or tasks.
•
Jobs may be executed interactively or batch processed.
•
Pending jobs are stored in queues. 
•
The job queue defines the order in which jobs are selected by the resource manager for execution.

SLURM
• Slurm Workload Manager (formely Simple Linux Utility for Resource Management 
(SLURM)) is an open-source, modular, extensible, scalable resource manager and workload 
scheduling software for clusters and supercomputers

--- page 21 ---
SLURM
Workload organisation
 
Nodes are divided into logical sets called partitions. 
 
Partitions in SLURM represent individual job queues and thus impose specific constraints on 
user jobs.
 
The scheduler assigns the available nodes  to the highest-priority jobs until the pool of available 
nodes is exhausted. 
 
The individual tasks composing a job, called job steps, may utilize the entire set of nodes 
allocated to the parent job or only a fraction.

--- page 22 ---
SLURM entities
• Nodes: Individual computers
• Partitions: Job queues
• Jobs: Resource allocations
• Job steps: Set of (typically 
parallel) tasks
