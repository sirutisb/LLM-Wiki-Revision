--- page 9 ---
Replication
• Each node stores a copy of the data (i.e., 
replica)
• Can reduce latency keeping data 
geographically close to the users
• Increase availability allowing the system 
to continue working even if some of its 
parts have failed 
• Increase performance scaling out the 
number of machines that can serve read 
queries
• Your data has to be small enough to be 
stored in a single machine
• Write operations involve updating all the 
copies of the data

--- page 10 ---
Leaders and followers
•
One of the replicas is designated the leader
Write operations are sent to a leader node which writes 
it to its local storage
•
The leader will send the update to the other 
replicas (i.e., followers) as part of a replication log. 
The followers will apply the same changes, in the same 
order, to their local copy of the data
•
Read queries can be sent to any replica while 
writing operations can only be sent to the leader
•
Built-in to many DBMSs like PostgreSQL, 
MongoDB, and MySQL

--- page 11 ---
Synchronous replication
• The leader will wait for the followers to 
confirm they received the write operation 
before reporting success
• Should they wait for all the followers to 
confirm success?
• Synchronous replication often means at 
least one follower is synchronous
If the synchronous follower becomes 
unavailable or slow, one of the asynchronous 
followers is made synchronous  (AKA semi-
synchronous)

--- page 13 ---
Adding new followers
• Take a snapshot of the leader’s database at some point in time
• Copy the snapshot to the new follower node. 
• The follower requests all the data changes that have happened since the snapshot was taken. 
• When the follower has processed the backlog of data changes, we say it has caught up.

--- page 14 ---
Recovering from failures
• Follower failure
From its logs, it knows the last processed transaction
Connect to the leader and request the data changes
• Leader failure (failover)
One of the followers will be promoted to the  new leader
• Often the replica with the most up-to-date data changes from the old leader 
Many problems can arise
• Split brain

--- page 15 ---
Replication lag
• Read- only queries can go to any replica 
• If an application reads from an asynchronous follower, it may see out-dated information 
• Many problems can arise from that
