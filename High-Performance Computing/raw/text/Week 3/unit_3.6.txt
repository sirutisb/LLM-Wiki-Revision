Stability
59
• We need to consider the stability of the numerical solution as well as the 
accuracy
• The solution must be guaranteed to remain finite for the scheme to be 
stable
• If errors in the solution can grow without bound the numerical scheme is 
unstable
• Carrying out a stability analysis is beyond the scope of this module but we 
will consider the factors which affect stability and types of stability

--- page 3 ---
• The stability of a numerical method depends on: 
              
- the equation being solved 
- how the spatial derivatives are calculated
- how the time steps are calculated

• A numerical method may be: 
              
- Unconditionally stable: errors are damped and the solution is always stable
- Unconditionally unstable: errors are amplified and the solution is always unstable
- Conditionally stable: solution is stable subject to a constraint
