Unit Summary
22
• Exponential decay occurs when a quantity decreases at a rate which is proportional to 
the quantity itself - radioactive decay is an example 
• Radioactive decay is described by a differential equation which gives rate of change of 
number of nuclei (Nt)
• The equation does not give us a value of Nt directly but instead tells us how Nt changes 
over time
• We can calculate a numerical solution by specifying an initial value and stepping 
forward in time 
• Larger time steps mean less computation but a less accurate result -there is a trade-off
