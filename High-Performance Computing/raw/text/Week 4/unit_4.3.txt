Collective communication

--- page 2 ---
• Using only point-to-point communication would be inconvenient for many operations, 
particularly with a large numbers of processes
• MPI also has operations for communicating within a group of processes specified by a 
communicator
• These are referred to as “collective operations” or “collectives”
• Collectives make MPI programming easier and can be more efficient than constructing 
the corresponding communication from point-to-point calls

--- page 17 ---
• In OpenMP there are reduction variables
• MPI has reduction functions which perform a similar role
• MPI_Reduce: carry out reduction and return result to the specified process
• MPI_Allreduce: carry out reduction and return result to all processes
• Communication overhead is greater if we return result to all processes

• MPI has collective communications which take place between the group of processes 
defined by a communicator 
• Examples of MPI collectives: 
- MPI_Bcast
- MPI_Scatter
- MPI_Gather/MPI_Allgather
- MPI_Reduce/MPI_Allreduce
• Collective communication patterns can be one-to-all, all-to-one or all-to-all
