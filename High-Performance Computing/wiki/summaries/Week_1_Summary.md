# Week 1 Summary: Introduction to HPC

## Overview
High Performance Computing (HPC) involves aggregating compute power to achieve high performance. Historically dominated by physical sciences and engineering (e.g., weather forecasting, computational fluid dynamics, astrophysics), it is increasingly vital in life sciences, particularly genomics.

## Key Concepts Introduced
*   **[High Performance Computing (HPC)](../concepts/High_Performance_Computing_HPC.md):** Aggregation of compute power.
*   **[Performance Metrics and Top500](../concepts/Performance_Metrics_and_Top500.md):** Measuring performance using FLOPs, Linpack benchmark, and tracking the fastest systems via the Top500 list.
*   **[Moore's Law and Dennard Scaling](../concepts/Moores_Law_and_Dennard_Scaling.md):** The need for parallelism as clock speeds stall while transistor density continues to increase.
*   **[Cluster Architecture](../concepts/Cluster_Architecture.md):** The dominant architecture, consisting of commodity off-the-shelf components.
*   **[HPC Programming Languages](../concepts/HPC_Programming_Languages.md):** Compiled languages like C, C++, and Fortran, augmented with parallel extensions like OpenMP and MPI.
