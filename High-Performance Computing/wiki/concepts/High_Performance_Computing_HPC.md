# High Performance Computing (HPC)

## Definition
High Performance Computing (HPC) involves aggregating computing power in a way that delivers much higher performance than one could get out of a typical desktop computer or workstation. It is used to solve large problems in science, engineering, or business.

## Applications
*   **Physical Sciences & Engineering (Traditional):** 
    *   Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD).
    *   Weather forecasting, climate change studies, oceanography (e.g., Met Office).
    *   Astrophysics (e.g., Star formation modeling, James Webb vs. Hubble data processing).
    *   Computational Engineering and Digital Twins.
*   **Life Sciences (Emerging):**
    *   Genomics. Characterized by being more data-orientated than traditional physical sciences HPC workloads.

## The Need for Parallelism
Calculations generate systems of equations that cannot be solved analytically. Numerical solutions require large amounts of floating-point operations. See [Moore's Law and Dennard Scaling](../concepts/Moores_Law_and_Dennard_Scaling.md) for why this requires parallel architectures.
